<p><p>The Fluorescent DNA Dye market features several key types, including Ethidium Bromide, Hoechst Dye, Propidium Iodide, and others. Ethidium Bromide is widely used for DNA visualization due to its ability to intercalate between base pairs. Hoechst Dye binds specifically to DNA, enabling the identification of live versus dead cells. Propidium Iodide is utilized primarily for cell cycle analysis, staining only dead cells. Other dyes in the market include SYBR Green, DAPI, and Acridine Orange, each catering to specific research and clinical applications.</p></p>
