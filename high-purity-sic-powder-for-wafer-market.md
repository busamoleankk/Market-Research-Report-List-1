<p><p>The High Purity SiC Powder for Wafer market is characterized by various production methods. The Self-propagating High-temperature Synthesis (SHS) method utilizes an exothermic reaction to create SiC efficiently. The Acheson method involves heating a mixture of silica and carbon in an electric furnace to achieve high purity. The Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) method uses gaseous precursors to produce thin films of SiC on substrates, providing controlled purity and structure. Each method serves distinct applications in semiconductor and electronic industries.</p></p>
