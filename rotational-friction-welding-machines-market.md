<p><p>Rotational friction welding machines are classified into several types based on their operational mechanisms. Inertia welding utilizes a rotating flywheel to store energy, which is released to create friction between materials. Direct drive welding features a motor directly connected to the workpieces, providing precise control. Hybrid systems combine elements of both inertia and direct drive for enhanced efficiency and flexibility. Other methods may include variations in design and functionality to cater to specific welding applications and materials.</p></p>
