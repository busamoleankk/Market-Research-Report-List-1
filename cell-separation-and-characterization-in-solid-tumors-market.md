<p><p>Cell separation and characterization in solid tumors encompass various techniques used to isolate and analyze specific cell populations within tumor samples. Fluorescence-Activated Cell Sorting (FACS) utilizes fluorescent markers for precise sorting. Magnet-Activated Cell Sorting (MACS) employs magnetic beads for cell isolation. Density Gradient Centrifugation separates cells based on density, while Dielectrophoretic methods utilize electrical fields. Aqueous Two-Phase Systems leverage phase partitioning for separation, and Field Flow Fractionation focuses on hydrodynamic forces for cell sorting. These techniques advance cancer research and treatment.</p></p>
